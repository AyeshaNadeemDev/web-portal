Soil, is a mixture of organic matter, minerals, gases, liquids, and organisms that together support life. It is a major component of ecosystem of the earth.About 45% of soil is made up of minerals, 5% of soil is organic matter, 20–30% of soil is water, and 20–30% of soil is air. The water supply, farming methods, and soil type are just a few examples of the many variables that can cause daily changes in the soil’s composition.Soil minerals are divided into three size classes, clay, silt,sand and the proportions of these particles make up the soil texture.

Soil Texture

The percentage of sand, silt, and clay-sized particles that make up the soil’s mineral component is referred to as the soil texture. The ability of nutrients and water to be maintained in the soil is influenced by the texture of the soil; as a result, clay and organic soils contain nutrients and water considerably better than sandy soils, where water drains and brings nutrients with it.The United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) has recognized 12 main categories for soil texture.The 12 classifications are sand, loamy sand, sandy loam, loam, silt loam, silt, sandy clay loam, clay loam, silty clay loam, sandy clay, silty clay, and clay. When describing the equal amounts of sand, silt, and clay in a soil sample, the term ”loam” is used.The percentages of sand, clay, and silt are represented by the triangle’s first, second, and third sides, respectively.A soil is categorised as a sandy loam if it contains 70% sand, 10% clay and %20 silt.

Electrical Conductivity

A measurement of the quantity of salts in soil is called soil Electrical Conductivity (EC). Electrical Conductivity and salinity of soil are interchangeable terms.It is a reliable measure of nutrient availability and loss as well as of soil texture and water capacity. The quantities of nitrates, potassium, sodium, chloride, sulphate, and ammonia have been associated with EC, despite the fact that it does not directly detect particular ions or salt compounds. Smaller clay particles conduct more electrical current than larger silt and sand particles, making soil EC a useful tool for determining the texture of the top layer.
